# Audio Bible

Sandbox Moto should sound like a cozy nostalgic memory.

## Music

Use minimal music. If present, keep it warm, sparse, and gentle. Avoid high-energy racing music.

## Ambience

- Birds.
- Wind.
- Distant dirt bikes.
- Kids laughing far away.
- Dog barking.
- Garage radio.
- Campground crickets.
- Parents talking in the distance.

## Tactile Sounds

- Plastic shovel scraping sand.
- Bucket dragging.
- Rake lines through dirt.
- Toy bulldozer pushing sand.
- Water dampening sand.
- Tiny flags tapping into dirt.

## Toy Race Sounds

Toy engine sounds should be tiny and imagined: small buzzes, soft hops, gentle skids, and playful crashes.

## Mom Called Dinner

"Mom called dinner" is a cozy save/pause moment. The world freezes, sunlight fades slightly, toy engines go quiet, and the sandbox is saved exactly as it was left.
