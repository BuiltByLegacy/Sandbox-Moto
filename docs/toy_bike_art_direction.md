# Toy Bike Art Direction

## North Star

The bikes should look like collectible toy motocross bikes that came alive in a kid's imagination.

Sandbox Moto bikes should feel inspired by nostalgic 1:12 scale toy motocross bikes, not real licensed motorcycles.

## Brand And Licensing Guardrails

Do not use real motorcycle brand names, logos, graphics, or exact model designs.

Avoid:

- Honda branding.
- Yamaha branding.
- Kawasaki branding.
- KTM branding.
- Husqvarna branding.
- Suzuki branding.
- Real factory graphics.
- Real race team replicas.
- Exact real motorcycle model silhouettes.

Use fictional toy-style brands and original graphics.

## Visual Target

The bikes should feel like premium toy store motocross bikes that a kid would race in a sandbox.

Key traits:

- 1:12 scale toy motocross bikes.
- Chunky plastic bodywork.
- Slightly oversized fenders.
- Thick toy-like tires.
- Simplified engines.
- Simplified forks and swingarms.
- Glossy molded plastic.
- Rounded toy-safe edges.
- Visible toy-like proportions.
- Plastic number plates.
- Simple decals.
- Tiny scratches and sand or dirt on tires.
- Collectible toy feel.

## Fictional Brand Examples

- Legacy Moto.
- Sandbox Racing.
- TrailWorks.
- DirtCo.
- MotoForge.
- TinyMoto.
- Backyard Factory.
- PitKid Racing.

These names are examples of the tone: original, playful, motocross-adjacent, and safe from real-world licensing.

## Colorways

Core colorways:

- Red.
- Blue.
- Green.
- Orange.
- Yellow.
- White.
- Black.
- Purple.

Special variants:

- Neon retro variants.
- Camo and pit bike variants.
- Snow day variants.
- Beach sand variants.

## Rider Art Direction

Riders should also look toy-like:

- Oversized helmets.
- Chunky boots.
- Simplified plastic body proportions.
- Colorful gear.
- Articulated action-figure feeling.
- No real rider names.
- No licensed gear.

The rider should read as part of the toy, not a realistic athlete.

## Animation Direction

The bikes should animate like toys brought to life by imagination:

- Slight bounce while riding.
- Simple wheelie over jumps.
- Whole-bike tilt in air.
- Simple landing bounce.
- Tiny roost or sand spray.
- Gentle wobble or spinout crashes.

Do not build toward full realistic suspension simulation.
