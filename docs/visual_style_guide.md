# Visual Style Guide

## Visual Target

Sandbox Moto should look and feel similar in spirit to Sandcastle:

- Cozy handcrafted sandbox diorama.
- Warm golden sand.
- Soft shadows.
- Miniature scale.
- Playful toy-like objects.
- Relaxing tactile building.

It should be motocross imagined through sandbox play, not gritty realistic motocross.

Creative references by spirit:

- Sandcastle-inspired cozy sandbox look.
- Tiny Glade-inspired relaxing creation and low-pressure shaping.
- Mini Motorways-inspired simple tool bubbles and editability.
- Handmade miniature diorama staging.
- 1:12 toy motocross bikes.

## Mood Words

- Cozy.
- Nostalgic.
- Handmade.
- Warm.
- Tiny.
- Playful.
- Relaxed.
- Tactile.

Everything should feel tiny:

- Toy riders.

- Cardboard ramps.

- Toy trees.

- Tiny spectators.
- Old tires.
- Toy spectators.

## Palette

Primary:

- Warm golden sand.
- Soft dirt brown.
- Sunlit clay.
- Faded backyard green.
- Bucket red.
- Warm golden lighting.

Accents:

- Toy-bike blue.
- Toy-bike red.
- Toy-bike green.
- Plastic yellow.
- Flag white and orange.

Avoid cold, gritty, high-contrast racing palettes.

## Materials

Sand and dirt should feel soft and shapeable. Track features should look hand-formed, with rounded edges and imperfect charm.

Lighting should use warm golden light and soft shadows. Plastic objects should have a toy plastic finish, with glossy molded highlights and rounded toy-safe edges.

Toy objects should be readable and slightly chunky:

- Bright toy bike bodies.
- Chunky tires.
- Simple number plates.
- Small cones and flags.
- Plastic bucket and shovel surfaces.

## Toy Dirt Bike Direction

The bikes should look like collectible toy motocross bikes that came alive in a kid's imagination.

Sandbox Moto bikes should feel inspired by nostalgic 1:12 scale toy motocross bikes, not real licensed motorcycles.

Important brand rule:

- Do not use real motorcycle brand names, logos, graphics, or exact model designs.
- Avoid Honda, Yamaha, Kawasaki, KTM, Husqvarna, and Suzuki branding.
- Use fictional toy-style brands and original graphics.

Visual targets:

- 1:12 scale toy motocross bikes.
- Chunky plastic bodywork.
- Slightly oversized fenders.
- Thick toy-like tires.
- Simplified engines.
- Simplified forks and swingarms.
- Glossy molded plastic.
- Rounded toy-safe edges.
- Visible toy-like proportions.
- Plastic number plates.
- Simple decals.
- Tiny scratches and sand or dirt on tires.
- Premium collectible toy-store feel.

The bikes should feel like premium toy store motocross bikes that a kid would race in a sandbox.

Fictional brand examples:

- Legacy Moto.
- Sandbox Racing.
- TrailWorks.
- DirtCo.
- MotoForge.
- TinyMoto.
- Backyard Factory.
- PitKid Racing.

Bike colorways:

- Red.
- Blue.
- Green.
- Orange.
- Yellow.
- White.
- Black.
- Purple.
- Neon retro variants.
- Camo and pit bike variants.
- Snow day variants.
- Beach sand variants.

Riders should also look toy-like:

- Oversized helmets.
- Chunky boots.
- Simplified plastic body proportions.
- Colorful gear.
- Articulated action-figure feeling.
- No real rider names or licensed gear.

## Toy Crowd

Spectators should be toys that make the race feel imagined:

- Army men.
- Dinosaurs.
- LEGO-style figures.
- Stuffed animals.
- Rubber ducks.
- Plastic animals.
- Toy trucks.

Use generic toy-like figures rather than licensed characters.

## Played-In Dirt Look

The sandbox should start to look played in:

- Darker ruts.
- Worn jump faces.
- Sand pushed out of corners.
- Smoother landings after repeated races.
- Tire marks.
- Little roost piles.

This should feel like an afternoon of play accumulating in the dirt.

## Camera Feeling

The camera should make the player feel like they are kneeling beside the sandbox. Later scenes can reveal the larger real-world setting around the tiny track.

## UI Direction

The UI should feel like play tools:

- Simple tool bubbles.
- Shovel, rake, bucket, stamp, and flag icons.
- Warm panels.
- No pro telemetry.
- No business dashboards.

## Avoid

- Gritty realism.
- Serious racing sim presentation.
- Stadium broadcast energy.
- Sponsor-heavy visuals.
- Mud-heavy simulation tone.
- Dark garage menus.
- Mechanical CAD/editor aesthetics.
- Real motorcycle brand names, logos, graphics, or exact model designs.
