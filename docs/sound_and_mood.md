# Sound And Mood

## Direction

Use minimal music and cozy nostalgic ambience.

Sandbox Moto should sound like a warm memory around motocross, toys, dirt, and backyard play.

## Ambience

Possible ambience layers:

- Birds.
- Wind.
- Distant real dirt bikes.
- Kids laughing far away.
- Dog barking.
- Campground crickets.
- Garage radio in winter map.
- Soft neighborhood sounds.
- Mom calling "Dinner!" as a rare memory moment.
- Dad's real dirt bike revving in the distance.

## Toy And Building Sounds

Building should sound tactile:

- Plastic shovel scraping sand.
- Bucket dragging.
- Rake lines in dirt.
- Toy bulldozer pushing sand.
- Small flags tapping into dirt.
- Soft sand smoothing.
- Water dampening sand.

## Rider Sounds

Toy bike engine sounds should be tiny and playful, not full realistic racing audio.

They can sound like:

- Small buzzy toy motors.
- Imagined mini engine noises.
- Gentle pops and hops.
- Tiny skids.
- Soft crash tumbles.

## Music

Music should be minimal. If used, it should be warm, light, and sparse. Avoid high-energy racing music.

## Mood Timing

The soundscape should support the loop:

- During Play Time, the world is mostly calm: sand tools, birds, wind, distant life.
- When Play is pressed, tiny toy engines and sand spray make the sandbox come alive.
- After the race, the toy engines fade and everything becomes still again.
